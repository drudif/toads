### The Importance of the Opening Seconds

The opening seconds for TikTok content (paid & organic) are make or break. Land the **CONTENT VALUE PROPOSITION** early, or risk losing the battle for consumer attention.

| Tempo | Referência |
|---|---|
| **0s** | Início do vídeo |
| **2s** | Land the content value proposition early |
| **6s** | 90% of the ad recall impact and 80% of the awareness impact is captured within 6 seconds |

TikTok supports three length formats: 15sec, 60sec and 3min. All these formats can find success, however research shows that regardless of format the first six seconds are vital for attention, impact and attribution.

**FIRST STEP:** Focus on your hook and work out where your brand/product can be additive to the content.

Source: TikTok, "Creative Guide: Driving Brand Equity", 2020 (TikTok "Value of View" Analysis).

## 6. Production

Fine tune your existing creative for TikTok through a three-step process:

- **RECUT** — Fine tune your existing production assets for TikTok
- **REMIX** — Remix creative assets to be more TikTok
- **REIMAGINE** — Reimagine your creative approach using the power of TikTok

**Production checklist:**
- Full-screen, vertical video
- Keep it high res
- Respect the safe zones
- Use creative tools (CapCut)
- Keep creative fresh
- Maximize shoots of all sizes

Source: TikTok Marketing Science Global Community and Self-Expression Study 2021 conducted by Flamingo.

### Creative Quantity & Diversity — Ad Group Level

**4+ creative assets** — A high number of creative assets allows for a better optimization of asset selection, allowing the system to quickly learn what works for each brand and each campaign. More creative assets in one ad group can bring higher impression rates, shelf life and cold start pass rate.

**8 days** — Assets to be refreshed every 8 days approx. Keep refreshing and uploading new video to your campaign until you find your winners. Data tells us that adding new creative assets, roughly, once every 8 days is the optimal practice.

Leverage Creative Tools such as TTCX for assistance with "remixing" existing creative to keep your brand's #ForYou content strategy fresh.

### Creative Asset Level

**Shoot high-res** — Ensure video resolution of 720P+. **312% lift in conversion** compared to other video resolutions.

**Go big with full-screen** — Stick to a 9:16 aspect ratio. **91% lift in conversion** compared to videos that are not formatted to screen (with empty black space on any of the sides).

**TikTok is sound-on by default** — Include audio. The more relevant, the better. **16% lift in impressions** compared to videos with no audio.

**The first few seconds are make-or-break** — 50% of total impact to recall and awareness occurs in the first 2 and 2.5 seconds, respectively. Optimal creative length is less definitive.

### Keep it fresh to increase engagement

| Abordagem | Tipo de Conteúdo |
|---|---|
| **RECUT** | Traditional Creative |
| **REMIX** | Native Creative |
| **REIMAGINE** | Creator Content / Community Audience Activation |

Lift from ads built natively & repurposed for TikTok: **+8% Brand Favorability**, **+6% Purchase Intent**, **+7% Search Intent**.

## Creative Cheatsheet

| Dimensão | Perguntas-chave |
|---|---|
| **TIKTOK FIRST** | Understanding TikTok First — what makes Branded content unique to TikTok? |
| **HOOK** | How do you hook your audience? What type of hook will you use? Can you credibly feature your brand or product in the hook? If you need to adapt existing assets, establish which editing tricks to use to appear TikTok First. |

| **SOUND** | What role will sound play? Will it be: Music, Voiceovers, Sounds. Check licenses (N/A with commercial music library). |

## Creative Thought Starters

### Behind the Scenes

Content that provides the community a peek behind the curtain on how things are made, run or done. For brands it also gives never-before-seen perspective, like how packages are packed to mail out to buyers, a look behind the scenes at a photoshoot, or a sneak peek into an upcoming drop.

How can your brand provide a unique perspective?

"After seeing that behind the scenes TikTok from Chipotle, I know why their guac is so addictive."

Featured TikTok: @niccilewis, a photographer, gives a behind the scenes look at photoshoot for Adidas.

---

### Content Series

A concept for a TikTok that is repeated over time, either as storytelling broken out across episodes or simply a repeated trope. TikToks can now be organized into Playlists, letting viewers easily find grouped content.

How might your brand's story be told over time? Do you have content that's doing well that could turn into a series?

"I am so invested in Lubalin's Internet Drama series. What song will he do next?"

Featured TikTok: @remibader does an Abercrombie haul where she tries the products she is unboxing.

---

### How To

A genre of TikTok that teaches viewers how to do something new, from how to do drag makeup, to how to tint car windows, to how to travel for free. "How-to" videos are category agnostic and show up in every niche community that exists on TikTok, including but not limited to sports and clothing.

How can you leverage "how-to" content to help your audience learn how to use your product? What skills or tips would help your audience meet their goals?

"I learned how to hide the cables behind my desk from watching a TikTok video!"

Featured TikTok: @timdessaint created a video on how to style Converse high tops.

---

### Occupation

Creators with different or unique occupations sharing their expertise and their unique daily routines. From social media managers to sales associates, TikTok offers a glimpse into different careers and jobs unknown to many.

Are there unique roles in the creation or implementation of your product you could share with TikTok?

"I've found a professional window cleaner on TikTok and I'm obsessed."

Featured TikTok: A video producer for American Eagle gives a peak into his work trip from Pittsburgh, PA to Anchorage, AK.

---

### POV

"Point of view" videos that showcase a unique or unexpected perspectives. They are often category agnostic, so they can show up across all the different TikTok communities. The traditional TikTok POV is shot from a first-person perspective, making the viewers the main character of the video. Many POVs are built on relatable moments, but with alt TikTok, the more surprising, niche or weird the POV, the better.

How can your brand use POV to communicate a specific customer perspective? Would you lean into a comedic angle with POV even if your brand doesn't typically do humor?

"I never thought I'd see the POV of my Lip Smacker lip gloss from 2002, but here we are."

Featured TikTok: Gap gives a peek into the perspective of a teacher and his students for the #GapToSchool campaign.

---

### Transition

Using clever editing and in-app camera tricks to create a surprising shift from one state to another. Often used to change scenes or outfits, common transitions include covering and uncovering the camera, fast swipes of the camera creating a blur, and positioning a new clip in the exact positioning of a previous clip.

How might transitions be used to show before-and-after brand experience?

"Not only do I love this Creator's outfits, I love the unique transitions that she creates."

Featured TikTok: Hollister worked with creator, @jeanvictorm, to shows off some outfits in a dance transition video.
